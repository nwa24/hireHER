Why do women feel discouraged when applying for certain jobs?

Gender-coded language

A study in 2011 shows that the way job ads are written may have a discriminatory effect even when there is no discriminatory intent. Masculine words were more likely to emerge within advertisements for male-dominated jobs, with a difference of 27%. There was no difference in the presence of female words across male-dominated and female-dominated jobs, with a difference of 10%. To promote gender-neutral hiring practices, our content editor- hireHER analyzes whether a job description contains subtle linguistic gender-coding that results in this discouraging effect. Our application takes in an imported pdf of the job description and by using NLP machine learning, the application identifies and highlights words that are statistically proven to be masculine. The user will then be shown a list of suggested gender-neutral replacement words that would reduce the bias within the description.
